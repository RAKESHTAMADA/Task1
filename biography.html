Virat Kohli

Virat Kohli (born 5 November 1988) is an Indian international cricketer and the former all-format captain of the Indian national cricket team. He is widely known for his remarkable batting ability and leadership skills. Kohli is a right-handed batter and also bowls occasionally with his right-arm medium pace. Over the years, he has built a reputation as one of the most consistent and dominant players in modern cricket. Many cricket experts, former players, and fans consider him one of the greatest all-format batsmen in the history of cricket. Because of his extraordinary performances and his ability to score runs in difficult situations, Kohli has been given several popular nicknames by fans and commentators. He is often called “King Kohli,” “Chase Master,” and “Run Machine.” These nicknames reflect his ability to control matches, score runs consistently, and guide his team to victory while chasing challenging targets. His aggressive approach to the game and his passion on the field have also made him one of the most recognizable and admired cricketers in the world. Kohli has achieved numerous milestones in international cricket. He holds the record for the most centuries in One Day Internationals (ODIs) and also has the second-highest number of centuries in international cricket, with 85 centuries across all formats. His ability to perform consistently in Test matches, ODIs, and T20 internationals has made him one of the most successful players of his generation. Apart from international cricket, Kohli has also made a significant impact in franchise cricket. He is the leading run-scorer in the Indian Premier League (IPL) and has delivered many memorable performances in the tournament. His dedication to his team and his consistent performances have made him one of the most valuable players in the league. Kohli is also recognized for his achievements as a captain in Test cricket. He is considered the most successful Test captain of India, having led the team to the highest number of Test victories. Under his leadership, the Indian team achieved remarkable success and managed to retain the ICC Test Championship mace three consecutive times, which was a significant achievement for the team during his captaincy. Another remarkable achievement in Kohli’s career is his exceptional batting ratings. He is the only batter to earn more than 900 rating points across all three formats of the game, which highlights his dominance and consistency in international cricket. This accomplishment demonstrates his ability to perform at the highest level in every format of the sport. Kohli first gained international attention when he captained the Indian team that won the 2008 ICC Under-19 Cricket World Cup. This victory marked an important moment in his early career and helped him gain recognition as a promising young cricketer. After that, he gradually became an important member of the Indian national team. During his international career, Kohli played a crucial role in several major tournament victories for India. He was an important part of the Indian team that won the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup, the 2013 ICC Champions Trophy, the 2024 ICC T20 World Cup, and the 2025 ICC Champions Trophy. His performances in these tournaments contributed significantly to India’s success and helped strengthen his reputation as one of the most dependable players in the team. In domestic cricket, Kohli represents Delhi, while in the IPL he plays for Royal Challengers Bengaluru. His long association with the franchise has made him one of the most iconic players in the history of the IPL. Kohli’s dominance in world cricket is also reflected in the ICC player rankings. In 2013, he was ranked number one in the ODI batting rankings. Later, in 2015, he reached the number one position in the T20 International rankings. In 2018, he achieved another milestone by becoming the number one ranked Test batsman in the world, making him the only Indian cricketer to hold the top position in all three formats of international cricket. Another remarkable record in Kohli’s career is that he became the first player to score 20,000 runs in a single decade. This achievement highlighted his extraordinary consistency and productivity with the bat throughout the 2010s. Because of his outstanding performances during that period, he was recognized as the Cricketer of the Decade for the years 2011 to 2020. Kohli has also received numerous awards and recognitions during his career. He has won ten ICC Awards, making him the most awarded player in the history of international cricket. Among these awards, he won the ICC ODI Player of the Year award four times in 2012, 2017, 2018, and 2023. He also won the prestigious ICC Cricketer of the Year award twice, in 2017 and 2018. In 2018, Kohli achieved a historic accomplishment by becoming the first player to win all three major ICC awards in the same year, including Cricketer of the Year, ODI Player of the Year, and Test Player of the Year. Later, in 2019, he was honored with the Spirit of Cricket Award, recognizing his sportsmanship and conduct on the field. In 2020, Kohli received further recognition when he was named ICC Cricketer of the Decade and ICC ODI Cricketer of the Decade for his exceptional performances throughout the decade. Additionally, he was named the Wisden Leading Cricketer in the World for three consecutive years, further confirming his status as one of the best players of his generation. Kohli also holds records for individual match awards. He has the highest number of Player of the Series awards and the second-highest number of Player of the Match awards across all three formats of international cricket combined. These achievements highlight his ability to deliver match-winning performances on a consistent basis. His contributions to Indian cricket have also been recognized by the Government of India. He received the Arjuna Award in 2013, the Padma Shri in 2017, and India’s highest sporting honour, the Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna Award in 2018. Kohli’s influence extends beyond the cricket field. In 2018, the international magazine Time included him in its list of the 100 most influential people in the world. His global popularity and commercial success have made him one of the most valuable athletes in the sports industry. In 2022, his estimated earnings were about ₹634 crore (US$75 million), which reflected his strong brand value and market presence. After helping India win the 2024 ICC T20 World Cup, Kohli played an important role in the final match and was awarded the Player of the Match. Following that historic victory, he announced his retirement from T20 International cricket. Later, on 12 May 2025, at the age of 36, Kohli also announced his retirement from Test cricket, bringing an end to another major chapter in his international career. In his personal life, Kohli is married to Indian actress Anushka Sharma. The couple has two children, a daughter named Vamika and a son named Akaay Kohli.

Early life

Virat Kohli was born on 5 November 1988 in New Delhi, India, into a Punjabi Hindu family. His family played an important role in shaping his early life and supporting his interest in cricket. His mother, Saroj Kohli, is a homemaker who took care of the household and the family, while his father, Prem Nath Kohli, worked as a criminal lawyer. Kohli grew up in a supportive family environment along with his siblings. He has an elder brother named Vikas and an elder sister named Bhawna, and the three siblings spent their childhood together in Delhi. During his childhood, Kohli spent most of his early years in Uttam Nagar, a locality in West Delhi. This area was where he grew up and developed his early interest in cricket. For his schooling, Kohli attended Vishal Bharti Public School, where he received his early education. According to his family members, Kohli showed a strong attraction toward cricket from a very young age. Even as a three-year-old child, he would often pick up a cricket bat and ask his father to bowl to him so that he could practice batting. This early passion clearly showed that he had a natural interest in the sport. In 1998, an important development happened when the West Delhi Cricket Academy was established. Recognizing their son's interest and talent in cricket, Kohli’s father decided to encourage him to pursue the sport more seriously. In May of that year, his father arranged a meeting between young Kohli and Rajkumar Sharma, who was associated with the academy. Neighbours also suggested that Kohli should join a professional cricket academy because they believed his talent deserved more than simply playing gully cricket in the streets. Taking this advice seriously, his father considered enrolling him in a professional training academy so that he could improve his cricketing skills under proper guidance. Despite his growing talent, Kohli faced some challenges during the early stages of his cricket career. At one point, he was unable to secure a place in the Delhi Under-14 team, reportedly due to external factors that were not related to his performance. During that time, Kohli’s father received offers from some influential clubs suggesting that they could relocate his son and guarantee his selection. However, his father decided not to accept these proposals. Instead, he chose to allow Kohli to continue progressing in his own way without relying on influence or unfair advantages. Eventually, Kohli managed to overcome these difficulties and earned a place in the Delhi Under-15 team. This opportunity helped him move forward in his cricket journey. During this time, he trained regularly at the academy and also participated in matches at the Sumeet Dogra Academy located in Vasundhara Enclave. These experiences helped him gain valuable match practice and develop his skills further. In order to give more time and focus to his cricket training, Kohli made an important decision during his school years. While studying in the ninth grade, he transferred from his earlier school to Saviour Convent School so that he could better balance his education and cricket training. This change allowed him to concentrate more seriously on building his cricket career. A major and emotional turning point in Kohli’s life occurred on 18 December 2006, when his father Prem Nath Kohli passed away due to a cerebral attack. This tragic event had a deep impact on him and his family. According to his mother, Kohli’s behaviour and attitude changed noticeably after his father’s death. He became more serious and focused about his future and his cricket career. After this difficult moment in his life, Kohli dedicated himself even more strongly to cricket. He began prioritizing practice, matches, and training sessions, showing a strong determination to succeed in the sport. His commitment and discipline increased significantly as he focused on improving his game and working hard to achieve his goals. For many years, Kohli’s family lived in Meera Bagh, Paschim Vihar, another area in Delhi. This remained their residence until 2015. After that year, the family relocated from Delhi to Gurgaon, marking another change in their living situation. These early experiences, including his childhood passion for cricket, the guidance of his father, his training at cricket academies, and the challenges he faced along the way, played an important role in shaping the cricketer he eventually became.

India U19 team

Virat Kohli continued to develop his cricket career through the India Under-19 team, where he began attracting attention for his consistent performances. In July 2006, Kohli was selected for the India Under-19 squad that toured England. During this tour, he delivered impressive performances in both formats of the game. In the three-match ODI series against England Under-19s, Kohli performed exceptionally well with the bat and achieved an outstanding average of 105, showing remarkable consistency. Alongside his success in the ODI matches, he also played in the three-match Test series, where he maintained a batting average of 49. These performances highlighted his ability to perform in different formats and conditions. Following the successful tour, the India Under-19 team won both the ODI and Test series against England Under-19s. After these victories, the team’s coach Lalchand Rajput praised Kohli’s performance and talent. Rajput specifically admired Kohli’s ability to play effectively against both pace bowling and spin bowling. He also spoke about Kohli’s strong technical skills and batting technique, recognizing that the young cricketer had the potential to achieve great success in the future. In September 2006, the India Under-19 team went on another international tour, this time visiting Pakistan. During the first Test match against Pakistan Under-19s, Kohli contributed important runs to the team’s success. He scored 63 runs in the first innings and 28 runs in the second innings, helping India secure a dominant 271-run victory over Pakistan Under-19s. His contributions in this match demonstrated his ability to perform in challenging international conditions. In the second Test match of the series, Kohli again played a valuable role for his team. He scored 83 runs, contributing significantly to India’s victory. India won that match by a large margin of 240 runs and an innings, highlighting the strength of the team during that tour. Kohli’s performance during this match further strengthened his reputation as a reliable young batsman. The tour concluded with a final ODI match in Lahore, where Kohli added 80 runs to his record. This performance further showed his ability to score important runs for his team and maintain consistency across different matches during the tour. In early 2007, Kohli continued to represent the India Under-19 team when the squad travelled to New Zealand. During this tour, he played an impressive innings in the first Test match, where he scored 113 runs, marking an important century in his early cricket career. The series between India Under-19 and New Zealand Under-19 eventually ended in a draw, but Kohli’s century was one of the notable highlights of the tour. In the following month, the India Under-19 team travelled to Malaysia to participate in a tri-series tournament involving England Under-19s and Sri Lanka Under-19s. During this series, Kohli did not get many opportunities to bat. As a result, he was unable to score many runs in that tournament. Despite the limited chances, he continued to remain a part of the team’s plans. Later, during July and August, the India Under-19 team embarked on another tour for a tri-series against Sri Lanka Under-19s and Bangladesh Under-19s. During this tournament, Kohli did not manage to score a half-century in any of the matches, which made the series relatively quiet for him compared to his earlier performances. However, Kohli soon made a strong comeback in the following Test series. In that series, he produced two excellent innings. He scored 144 runs in one match and followed it with an unbeaten 94 runs in another innings. These performances demonstrated his resilience and ability to bounce back after a challenging series. Kohli gained major recognition in 2008, when he came into the international spotlight after leading the Indian team to victory in the Under-19 Cricket World Cup. He served as the captain of the Indian squad during the tournament, which was held in Malaysia. Under his leadership, India successfully won the 2008 ICC Under-19 World Cup, marking an important milestone in his early cricket career. During the tournament, Kohli performed well with the bat and scored a total of 235 runs, maintaining an average of 47. His performance made him the third-highest run scorer of the tournament. He was also one of the three players who managed to score a century during the competition. One of Kohli’s most memorable performances came during a group stage match against West Indies Under-19s. In that match, he played an outstanding innings of 100 runs from just 74 balls. This impressive century was highly praised by cricket analysts and commentators. The cricket website ESPNcricinfo even described the knock as “the innings of the tournament.” Kohli’s century played a crucial role in helping India win that match by 50 runs, and his outstanding performance earned him the Man of the Match award. This innings further established his reputation as a promising young cricketer with great potential. In the semi-final match against New Zealand Under-19s, Kohli delivered an important all-round performance. He contributed with both bat and ball during the match. With the ball, he took two wickets, and with the bat he scored 43 runs while chasing the target. His efforts were vital in helping India secure victory and reach the final of the tournament. In the final match of the tournament, India faced South Africa Under-19s. During this match, Kohli scored 19 runs. Although his score was modest, his leadership as captain helped guide the team to success. India eventually won the championship match by 12 runs through the Duckworth–Lewis method, securing the Under-19 World Cup title. In June 2008, Kohli and two of his Under-19 teammates, Pradeep Sangwan and Tanmay Srivastava, received the Border–Gavaskar Scholarship. This scholarship was designed to help young cricketers improve their skills and gain international training experience. As part of the scholarship program, the selected players were given the opportunity to train for six weeks at Cricket Australia's Centre of Excellence in Brisbane. Around the same time, selectors were looking for young players who could potentially represent the senior Indian national team in the future. As part of this effort to identify emerging talent, Kohli was selected to represent India Emerging Players in the Emerging Players Tournament 2008. During this tournament, Kohli delivered one of his finest performances against New Zealand Emerging Players. In that match, he played a brilliant innings of 120 runs, leading India to a comfortable seven-wicket victory. His batting performance was one of the standout moments of the tournament. By the end of the competition, Kohli had accumulated a total of 204 runs, which caught the attention of the selectors who were closely monitoring his progress. His consistent performances at the youth and emerging levels demonstrated that he had the potential to move to the next stage of his career.Shortly afterward, Kohli began his international cricket journey when he joined the senior Indian national team in Colombo. By that time, he had already gained considerable experience, having played 28 Under-19 ODIs and 12 Under-19 Test matches, which helped prepare him for the challenges of international cricket.

Personal Life

Virat Kohli's romantic association with Bollywood actress Anushka Sharma began in 2013. Over time, their relationship became widely known and attracted considerable attention from fans, media outlets, and the general public. Because of their popularity as individuals and as a couple, the pair came to be popularly referred to by the nickname “Virushka,” a combination of their names. This name quickly became widely used by fans and the media when referring to their relationship. During an interview with Graham Bensinger, Kohli revealed details about how he first met Anushka Sharma. He explained that the two first encountered each other while participating in a promotional shoot for the brand Clear shampoo. At the time, both Kohli and Sharma were already well-known public figures in their respective professions. Their meeting during the advertisement shoot eventually led to the beginning of their relationship. From that point onward, their association began to receive a great deal of media attention. As their relationship continued, it attracted significant coverage in newspapers, television programs, and online media platforms. There were frequent rumours, speculations, and discussions in the press about their relationship status. Despite the continuous attention from the media, both Kohli and Sharma chose to remain relatively private about their personal lives. They did not publicly discuss many details about their relationship and generally maintained a reserved approach when it came to addressing speculation in the press. On 11 December 2017, the couple officially got married. Their wedding ceremony was conducted in an intimate setting in Florence, Italy, and it was attended by close family members and a small group of friends. Even though the ceremony itself was private, the wedding quickly became one of the most talked-about celebrity events in India. The union of Kohli and Sharma further strengthened their public image as one of the most prominent and admired celebrity couples in the country. A few years after their marriage, the couple welcomed their first child. On 11 January 2021, Kohli and Sharma became parents to a daughter, whom they named Vamika. The birth of their daughter was widely celebrated by fans and received significant attention in the media. Later, the family grew once again when the couple welcomed their second child, a son named Akaay, on 15 February 2024. Apart from his personal life, Kohli has also spoken openly about certain lifestyle changes he made for health reasons. In 2018, he revealed that he had decided to adopt a vegetarian diet. This decision was made in order to help alleviate symptoms related to a cervical spine issue, which had developed due to elevated levels of uric acid in his body. The condition was affecting his finger movements, which in turn was impacting his ability to perform effectively as a batsman. To address this health issue, Kohli decided to make significant adjustments to his diet and lifestyle. One of the major steps he took was to stop consuming meat, choosing instead to follow a vegetarian diet as part of his efforts to maintain better health and improve his physical condition. By adopting this dietary change, he aimed to reduce the symptoms associated with the cervical spine problem and ensure that his physical performance remained at a high level. However, Kohli has clarified that although he follows a vegetarian diet, his eating habits do not fully align with a vegan lifestyle. He continues to consume dairy products, which means that his dietary practices are vegetarian rather than vegan. This distinction has been mentioned by Kohli himself when discussing his lifestyle choices. Kohli is widely recognized for his exceptional physical fitness and intense training routine. Throughout his career, he has been known for maintaining a disciplined approach toward fitness. His daily routine typically involves regular exercise, structured workouts, and strict attention to nutrition. Because of his commitment to physical training and maintaining a healthy diet, Kohli has developed a reputation for being one of the fittest cricketers in the world. His dedication to fitness has also influenced many young athletes and cricket fans, who view him as a role model for maintaining a healthy and disciplined lifestyle. Kohli has frequently spoken about the importance of exercise, balanced nutrition, and physical conditioning in sustaining a successful sports career. His hard work and consistent effort in maintaining his physical condition have been key factors in his long-lasting success in international cricket. In addition to his disciplined lifestyle, Kohli has also acknowledged that he holds certain personal beliefs in superstitions related to cricket. Like many athletes, he follows a number of rituals and practices that he believes bring him good luck on the cricket field. These superstitions form a small but notable part of his personal routine before and during matches. Among the practices he follows is the habit of wearing a black wristband, which he considers to be a lucky item. He is also known to use a single pair of gloves during matches, which he regards as another element of his personal routine. These small rituals have become part of his on-field identity and are often noticed by fans and commentators during matches. Another visible item that Kohli frequently wears is a kara, a traditional metal bangle that is often associated with religious or spiritual beliefs. Kohli has been seen wearing this kara on his right arm since 2012, and it has remained a constant part of his appearance both on and off the cricket field. In addition to these beliefs and rituals, Kohli has also developed the habit of consistently wearing white shoes during cricket matches. This has become another personal tradition that he follows while playing on the field. Kohli is also known for having several tattoos, each of which holds personal meaning for him. Among these tattoos is one depicting the Hindu deity Shiva, reflecting his spiritual beliefs. He also has tattoos that include the names of his parents, symbolizing the importance of family in his life. In addition, Kohli has tattoos representing his ODI and Test match cap numbers, which serve as reminders of important milestones in his cricket career.
